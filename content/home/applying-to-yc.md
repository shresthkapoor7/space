Applied to YC on a caffeine-fueled whim. No co-founder, no traction, just a working demo and a bet that the idea was worth something.

## The idea

Every LLM app has the same problem: context rot. You ask one off-track question in ChatGPT and the whole conversation drifts. You end up with hallucinations, lost threads, and no way back.

[Strands](https://www.strandschat.com) fixes this by letting you branch a conversation from any message - the same way Slack threads work. Each branch gets its own isolated context window. Explore a tangent, close it, and your main thread is exactly where you left it. Better UX on the surface, cleaner architecture underneath.

## What happened

Didn't get in. Expected, honestly - solo founder, early stage, no revenue. But the process forced me to articulate the idea clearly for the first time, which was worth it on its own.

Still building. The backend is getting a full Docker rewrite, the frontend is getting cleaner, and the branching UX is getting sharper. Not done yet.
